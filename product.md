product_vision: Project Dwayne

user_persona:
  identity: "Ishaan, 36. Former startup founder turned senior product leader at a global technology company. Father of two young boys. Mid-career athlete — not a beginner, not a professional. Has built and shipped products used by millions; now applies the same precision to rebuilding his body in a fixed 7-week window."
  psychographics: "Values proof over effort, evidence over intention. Deep aversion to generic tools that weren't built for him — the imprecision of using something designed for everyone feels like a personal insult. Motivated by the clock: there is a fixed date, a fixed number of days, a known target weight and body composition. Hates wasted potential in himself above all else. Distrusts vague progress. Needs to see the gap between where he is and where he needs to be — both as fuel and as accountability."
  context: "5:30am in a gym — hotel or commercial, wherever he is that week. Phone in hand, forty minutes before the day starts in earnest. Mentally sharp but time-compressed. The training plan is designed and fixed; the exact load for this phase, the cardio target for today, what to eat and when — these details need to be immediately available without deliberation."

problem_statement:
  trigger: "It is the first day of a new training phase. The loads have progressed. The macro target is different because today is a training day. The supplement timing has shifted. He is standing in the gym and cannot hold all of it cleanly in his head."
  friction: "Generic fitness apps were built for people who are designing their program as they go. They force him to recreate a fixed plan inside their structure, or ignore half of what they do. A spreadsheet does not travel well, does not push accountability, and requires mental arithmetic he has already done. Nothing holds the complete plan — the sessions, the nutrition, the supplements, the weekly body checks — and accepts today's log with minimum friction."
  consequence: "Without a tool that holds the plan and surfaces exactly what each day demands, he either improvises (imprecision that compounds over 49 days) or burns cognitive energy on logistics he has already solved (waste that erodes motivation). Worse: without visible, structured progress — weight trending across weeks, photos changing side by side — the internal belief that the work is producing results decays before the results are visible. He risks abandoning a programme that is working because he cannot see that it is working."
  why_now: "The 7-week window is fixed and already started. There is no version where he figures out the tool later. The plan is designed; compliance is the only variable left."

ideal_user_journey:
  1: "He opens the app before the first set. The day is already decided — session type, exercises and loads for this phase, cardio target, water goal, what to take before training. There is nothing to figure out. He reads it and gets to work."
  2: "He trains. Afterwards, he logs the session in under a minute: how much he lifted, how the session felt. For cardio, a structured check — intervals completed, heart rate. No more, no less."
  3: "Through the day, he ticks off supplements as he takes them, logs meals, taps water up. Not because the system demands it — because he can see the gap between where he is and where he needs to be before the day ends."
  4: "On Monday morning he steps on the scale and takes a photo. The app records both alongside the previous weeks — a visual line of weight and a row of body silhouettes, each week either confirming the trajectory or demanding an honest reckoning."
  5: "On June 3rd, the record is complete. 49 sessions logged, body composition tracked from start to finish, the full arc visible. He did not just train — he documented, and the proof exists."

emotional_arc:
  1: "Oriented (The app removes all ambiguity — the day is decided before he walks in)"
  2: "Accountable (Logging makes the gap between plan and reality visible — honest and deliberately uncomfortable)"
  3: "Satisfied (Each completed log, ticked supplement, hit water target is a small confirmation that the day happened as designed)"
  4: "Compelled (The weekly photo and weight trend create productive pressure — the work is either showing or it is not, and either truth is useful)"
  5: "Proud (A complete record exists. Not a memory of effort, but evidence of what was built and what it produced)"

stakeholder_impact:
  end_users: "The plan stops living in his head and starts living in an artefact he can trust. He finishes 7 weeks knowing exactly what he did, when, and what it produced — the difference between a transformation remembered vaguely and one documented with precision."
  business: "Proof of concept for hyper-personalised, single-user health tools that refuse to compromise on precision for the sake of mass-market appeal. Validates that the most effective product for one specific person is often built by that person, to their exact specification — and that the result is categorically better than anything designed for everyone."
